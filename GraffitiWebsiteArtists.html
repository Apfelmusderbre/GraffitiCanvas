Legal Artists:

Legal artists are individuals who create graffiti or street art with the permission of property owners or relevant authorities. Their work is sanctioned and aligns with local regulations, fostering a positive relationship between artists and the community. Legal artists often collaborate with businesses, municipalities, or property owners to beautify public spaces.

Blade (Steven Ogburn)

Blade is a legendary graffiti artist known for his involvement in the New York City graffiti scene during the 1970s and 1980s. He is recognized for his mastery of traditional graffiti styles and vibrant colors.

Futura 2000 (Leonard Hilton McGurr)

Futura 2000 is an American graffiti artist who emerged in the early 1970s. He is known for his abstract, futuristic style that has influenced both graffiti and contemporary art. Futura 2000 has successfully transitioned from the streets to galleries.

Daze (Chris Ellis)

Daze is a New York-based artist who gained prominence in the 1970s for his graffiti work on subway trains. He is known for his letter-based style and has also exhibited in galleries around the world.

Lady Pink (Sandra Fabara)

Lady Pink is a pioneer in the world of graffiti art and one of the few prominent female graffiti artists. She has been active since the 1970s and is known for her intricate lettering and colorful murals.

SEEN (Richard Mirando)

SEEN is a Bronx-born graffiti artist who began his career in the early 1970s. He is recognized for his distinctive lettering style and has played a significant role in the evolution of graffiti as an art form.

Revok (Jason Williams)

Revok is a contemporary graffiti artist known for his involvement in both legal and illegal graffiti. His work often combines traditional letter-based elements with abstract and geometric forms.

Tats Cru

Tats Cru is a group of Bronx-based graffiti artists, including Bio, BG183, and Nicer. They are known for their collaborative murals and traditional graffiti styles that incorporate vibrant colors and intricate lettering.

POSE (Jordan Nickel)

Pose is a contemporary artist known for his transition from illegal graffiti to legal mural work. His letter-based style often includes bold colors and dynamic compositions.

Illegal Artists:

Illegal artists, also known as taggers or vandals, engage in graffiti without proper authorization or consent. Their activities may violate local laws and regulations, leading to legal consequences. While some illegal artists may express themselves as a form of protest or rebellion, their actions can contribute to negative perceptions of graffiti as a whole.

Notice: Graffiti Artist Information Policy

As a responsible and law-abiding community, we do not endorse or provide information about illegal graffiti artists. Our platform is committed to promoting legal and ethical forms of artistic expression.

However, we encourage discussion and documentation of renowned street artists, legal mural projects, and street art festivals. If you are a member of our community and have information about legal graffiti artists, feel free to contribute to our collaborative effort. Members with certain ranks are allowed to edit and add information about recognized and respected artists within the boundaries of legality and ethical practices.

Let's celebrate the positive impact of urban art within legal and constructive contexts. Thank you for your understanding and cooperation.

Street Art Artists:

Street art artists encompass a broad spectrum, including legal and illegal practitioners, who use public spaces as their canvas. Street art often transcends traditional graffiti, incorporating various styles and mediums such as stencils, murals, and installations. Street art is a dynamic and evolving form of artistic expression that can convey social, political, or cultural messages.

Aiko Nakagawa (Lady Aiko)

A Japanese street artist based in New York, Lady Aiko combines traditional Japanese imagery with contemporary pop art, creating vibrant and dynamic pieces often featuring strong female figures.

Banksy

Perhaps the most famous street artist globally, Banksy's identity remains a mystery. His politically charged and thought-provoking stencils often carry a satirical message.

Blu

An Italian street artist recognized for his large-scale murals that often feature surreal and thought-provoking imagery. Blu's work can be both whimsical and deeply contemplative.

D*Face (Dean Stockton)

A British street artist known for his pop-art-inspired work. D*Face often incorporates bold colors and comic book aesthetics to explore themes of celebrity, consumerism, and mortality.

Faith47 (Graffiti name)

A South African street artist whose work often delves into social issues, spirituality, and the environment. Faith47's murals are known for their emotional depth and poetic qualities.

Invader

A French street artist who gained fame for his pixelated mosaic art inspired by 8-bit video games. Invader's work can be found in cities worldwide, and he gives points to those who document and photograph his installations.

JR

A French artist known for his large-scale photographic murals that often tackle social and political issues. His "Inside Out" project encourages people to share their portraits and stories.

Os Gemeos

Brazilian twin brothers Otavio and Gustavo Pandolfo are known for their vibrant and surreal characters. Their work often reflects Brazilian culture and folklore, combining graffiti with street art.

ROA

A Belgian street artist celebrated for his large-scale murals featuring detailed and often monochromatic depictions of animals. His work can be found on buildings in cities across the globe.

Shepard Fairey

Known for the iconic "Hope" poster during Barack Obama's 2008 presidential campaign, Fairey's work often combines street art with activism, featuring bold graphics and social commentary.

Swoon (Caledonia Dance Curry)

An American street artist whose intricate paper cutouts and wheatpaste prints often depict human figures. Swoon's work often explores themes of community and empathy.

Vhils (Alexandre Farto)

Hailing from Portugal, Vhils is recognized for his unique technique of carving and chiseling into urban surfaces like walls and billboards to create textured and expressive portraits.

Embrace the diversity of urban art and join us in celebrating the boundless creativity that transforms the ordinary into the extraordinary.
